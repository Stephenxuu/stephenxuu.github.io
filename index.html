About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Data Analytics and Decision Science at the Darden School of Business, University of Virginia, advised by Prof. Manel Baucells and Prof. Sasa Zorc.

My research develops theory for optimal sequential decision-making under uncertainty, with a focus on search theory, Bayesian learning, bandits, and data-driven pricing. A running theme is applying these frameworks to inference-time computation for large language models and to behavioral decision making. Before my Ph.D., I received an M.A. in Applied Economics from the National University of Singapore and worked as a Product Manager at Tencent and as a Research Associate at CUHK‑Shenzhen.

I serve as a referee for Operations Research and Management Science.
